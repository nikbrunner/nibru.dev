Somewhere between 2012 and 2013, i worked in the sound industry. During the quiet phases in the studio when there was not much to do, I wanted to fill the time with something meaningful. And since I have been less and less involved with my own music production since my education as a sound engineer, I wanted to learn something new.

The first thing that interested me about photography was not the photos themselves, but the numbers that were always associated with them... F8, 1/230s... What do these numbers mean?'.. I asked myself. It was a completely different world with completely different units, and since I learned through sound engineering that units are always the key to certain results, I became curious. I've been interested in visual media since Star Wars. Photos, illustrations, or movies were often windows to the imagination of other people and worlds. Many of my generation grew up as 'screenagers' and lived through the early days of the Internet, when my friends and me dialed in together over one 56K modem.

However, since i earned enough money at the time, i quickly bought a Canon Entry Level Camera, and started to photography everything around me. Until today, I still have to clean out big numbers of trash photos from back than. But it was a lot of fun and a whole new world. I dived in deep and looked up tutorials, podcasts and other forms of media, and nearly everyday after work i went out with my camera. I started to photograph my family, my girlfriend and made a neat photo-book for every vacation we went on.

A few years later, when i switched from the studio job to be a event technician, i was very unhappy. At the time i tried to get out of the event technology business, by trying to make it as a photographer. I shot a few weddings, ceremony's, concerts and other stuff like yoga clients. The money was okay, but i didn't like doing it very much.

Although all my clients, with one exception, were always very happy with the results, I often wasn't myself. It often seemed boring to me and I had my own ideas, which I often could not or was not allowed to fulfill. After some jobs and despite further inquiries, I decided not to pursue photography commercially. Since the motifs I wanted to photograph were very close to my heart and I wanted to present them as I would like to see them again later, it was more important to me to have fun with photography and to realize my ideas in my free time.

Today i almost exclusively shoot landscapes, sometimes a some cityscapes or street photography and some portraiture. Portraiture means in 99% of all cases pictures of my fiance.

Although i even despised landscape photography in the beginning (why bother taking a photo of something not moving right?), i learned to love it. Since I always felt the most comfortable in nature, i realized that the combination of photography and nature is a really great thing. You really take your time and you look at nature with different eyes. I slows and calms you down, and every time i am out there hiking, i forget everything else.
